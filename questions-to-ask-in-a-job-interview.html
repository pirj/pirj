@═╦╣╚╗ A mazing engineer 26-Apr-2020 Questions to Ask in a Job Interview Comment on this article I don't have a habit to plan or prepare for interviews. Company's engineering blog reveals challenges and openness to share best practices. Company's GitHub account shows quality standards. That's all I know before the interview. But the question "do you want to know something about the company" during an interview still puzzles me. If you're like me, I've compiled a list of questions to ask. They unveil distinctive factors that may affect your choice whether to proceed with them or not. Why? Answers to those questions come to you sooner or later when you join a company. And often times you can't change or handle that truth. Those questions can reveal the factors that make your daily job a breeze, or a nightmare. Some answers are better to be known before making a serious decision. If you are primarily motivated by the salary, those questions are NOT for you. But are you really? Disclaimer: I'm a backend Ruby developer, YMMV. However, the language isn't playing such a big role those days. Operational, administrative, organizational, and architectural questions still apply for companies using other platforms. Those questions are dry. You ought to understand why the question has to be asked and pros and cons of major possible answers. Profiling and psychological tests intend to ask the same things in different ways. The questions below have similar interlinked traps that may reveal the real state of affairs in your prospective employer. The time frame for interviewee's question is quite limited. Make sure that the interviewer knows in advance that you have a fair amount of questions. Ask what matters most to you. Choose wisely. Consider this list as a compliment and an update to Joel Test and Joel Test for 2017. Warmup Please briefly tell about how an idea of a change progresses towards a release to production. Do you have an engineering blog? What databases are in use and assessment, and why? Do you prefer "boring" old technologies or do you follow the new wave? What in my public profile made you think I may be a good fit for your company and project? Organization How many people work in the engineering department? What are their role titles? How do those roles communicate? How many people are there in the team? Communication How do you communicate in the team? How fast an employee is expected to react to mail and instant messenger? What other services do you use in the team? What meetings are mandatory for the team? How long do they take out of the working hours? Do you assess meeting efficiency and necessity? How friendly and willing to assist coworkers are? Engineering What's the level of developers in my future team and other teams? What's the level of trust towards regular engineers in terms of supervision? Do you have any algorithmic or math tasks in your daily job? Is backend, frontend, test automation, and devops code written by the same people? Do you have engineering duty? How often? What does it encompass? What's the worst duty accident you can remember? What's your release cycle like? How up to date is your toolset? Knowledge How do you document best practices and guidelines, is it written down or is spread by the word of mouth? How detailed and unambiguous engineering documentation is? Where engineering documentation is kept? Is it easy to search and change? How do you know who's the most knowledgeable person given a topic? How do you deal with the bus factor? How do new hires learn what they need to know to become productive contributors? Do you have a new hire playbook? How the guidelines and best practices for different areas of the product are written? Do you practice mentoring? Is it only for the new hires, or is it used to improve skills of less experienced engineers? Architecture How do you build frontend code? How does your frontend communicate with backend? What languages and frameworks you consider as an option when starting a project from scratch? What is used for DevOps? Local Development Do you have any specific tools that require specific hardware or operating system? Where the data for local development comes from? Is it real, or fake? Code Review Do you review every code and documentation change? What are your code reviews like? How many rounds of code reviews do you typically have for a big change? How many people are involved in a code review? What happens if (just) one of the code reviewers is totally not happy about some part of the change? Code Quality How do you deal with bugs, what are the priorities, and how they are assessed? How do you deal with technical debt? Who's responsible to prioritize it? Are your tests fast? Are they reliable? Are they trustworthy? How do you document the code? How do you deal with badly documented, poorly covered with tests, unreadable code that is below your average? How do you deal with legacy code? How do you deal with complicated database queries, do you use raw SQL, advanced framework features, or stored procedures? What checks are performed locally and on CI? Open Source Are you a consumer only, or do you pay back in some way to the open source community? How motivated your employees are to work on open source during working hours. Is it only allowed to work on open source during working hours on things that are blocking or complicating the daily job? Is it only allowed to work on open source during working hours on projects that are directly or distantly related to the daily job? Do you understand the importance and benefits of sharing the code and practices with the public? Are there some dedicated hours, days, sprint points, or employees for open source? What are the conditions to be met for an employee to work on an open source project during working hours? Development Process How does a product change request look like when it reaches the developer? To what extent is it possible for a developer to affect this change request? Do you use some development process? Why this one, not X? How well the flavour of this development process is documented? To what extent a team can make amendments to the development process to fit their needs and improve their pipeline? How hard the product pressure is? Career Development How transparent are performance reviews and rules behind it? How do performance reviews affect the paycheck? How to go up in terms of your role and responsibilities? How do you deal with the turnover? How your turnover compares with other companies? Do you have a percentile graph of employee engagement length with the company in the engineering department? How fast do you grow? What does the firing/quitting process looks like? (macro)-Services How do you locally run dependent services? How do you synchronize releases? How do you draw the line between those services? Is the database between services shared? Do they have dedicated databases? Do they overlap? What do you use to imitate interservice communication? How are the interservice protocols documented? How are the interservice communications tested? Microservices Alright, you're using microservices or are tearing apart your monolithic app. What problems did you have you decided to solve with microservices? Have you considered alternative approaches to those problems? How do you deal with the problems of: - consistency - availability - M*N emitter to consumer communication (note: "the bus" isn't an answer) - message log retention - message schema versioning - stream and table dualism - keeping an actual dimension of data - data store separation - interservice join queries - deploy synchronization - transactional atomic updates of data spread over several services Testing Do you perform manual testing? Do you have a dedicated QA engineer in the team? How thorough is the manual testing? When manual testing is happening? What do you cover with automated acceptance tests? Do you perform automated load testing? Do you perform penetration testing? DevOps Do regular developers have access to production logs and console? Who's responsible for deployments? How do you automate deployments? How long does the deployment take? Can you perform a quick lossless rollback? Can you deploy a hotfix? Do you use zero-downtime deployment? Do you use atomic and fast schema migrations? How do you deal with data migrations? How do you run data migrations interleaved with schema migrations on deployment? Incident Management Who's receiving production and staging error messages? How do they react to those messages? Culture What's the culture of the company? Does engineering department have its own culture? To what extent the culture applies to everything that company does in reality? What are the exceptions? Did you grow the culture? Or is it an inapproachable ideal you are struggling to reach? How do you teach your employees why your culture is good? Do you teach your employees to become good at doing it the company way? How do you deal with those who makes an unintentional mistake that costs the company its profit or increases maintenance costs? Under which circumstances is it possible to work on your own nonprofit or profit projects in your spare time? Next time you want to hire me, please prepare the answers.
