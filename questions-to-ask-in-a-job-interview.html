  26-Apr-2020 Questions to Ask in a Job Interview

  Comment on this article

  I never plan or prepare for interviews, so a question "do you want to know something about the company" always puzzles me. The same happens when a recruiter reaches out to you with a proposal from an unnamed company. Is it so important for you to know the company name? You'll have to dig for all the details in the company blog and various feedback sites. Not fun.

  If you're like me, I've compiled a list of questions to ask. They cover common differences between companies that really make sense for you as a perspective employee.

  Disclaimer: I'm a back-end Ruby developer, so YMMV. However, the language isn't playing such a big role those days. Operational, administrative, organizational, and architectural questions still apply for other platforms.

  Warning: Those questions are dry, you may not have enough experience to assess the answer, or have a good context what it affects. Some questions imply that you know pros and cons of major possible answers.

  There are some interlinked traps in questions that may reveal the real state of affairs in your prospective employer.

  The time frame for interviewee's question is usually quite limited. Make sure to warn the interviewer that you have a fair amount of questions upfront.

  It's up to you what matters most, and what to ask. Choose wisely.

  Consider this list as a compliment and an update to Joel Test and Joel Test for 2017.

  Warm-up

    Please briefly tell about how an idea of a change progresses towards being deployed to production.

    Do you have an engineering blog?

    What databases are in use and assessment, and why?

    Do you prefer "boring" old technologies or do you follow the new wave?

    What in my public profile made you think I may be a good fit for your company and project?

  Organization

    How many people work in the engineering department?

    What are their role titles?

    How do those roles communicate?

    How many people are there in the team I'll join?

  Communication

    How do you communicate in the team?

    How fast an employee is expected to react to mail and instant messenger?

    What other services do you use in the team?

    What meetings, I'll be having in the team, how long do they take out of my working hours and why?

    Do you assess meeting efficiency and necessity?

    How friendly and willing to help coworkers are?

  Engineering

    What is the level of developers in my future team and other teams?

    What is the level of trust towards regular engineers in terms of supervision?

    Do you really have any algorithmic/math-related tasks in your daily job?

    Is back-end, front-end, test automation, and devops code written by the same people?

    Do you have engineering duty? How often? What do you typically do?

    What is the worst duty in the company you can remember?

    What is your release cycle like?

  Knowledge

    How do you document best practices and guidelines, is it a written text or "just known by everyone how it is"?

    How detailed and non-ambiguous engineering documentation is?

    Where engineering documentation is kept? Is it easy to search and change?

    How do you know who is the most knowledgeable person given a topic?

    How do you deal with the bus factor?

    How do new hires learn what they need to know to become productive contributors?

    Do you have a new hire playbook?

    How the guidelines and best practices for different areas of the product are written?

    Do you practice mentoring? Is it only for the new hires, or is it used to improve skills of less experienced engineers?

  Architecture

    How you build front-end code?

    How does your front-end communicate with back-end?

    What languages and frameworks you consider as an option when starting a project from scratch?

    What is used for DevOps?

  Local Development

    Do you have any specific tools that require specific hardware or operating system?

    Where the data for local development comes from? Is it real, or fake?

  Code Review

    Do you review every code and documentation change?

    What are your code reviews like?

    How many rounds of code reviews do you typically have for a big change?

    How many people are involved in a code review?

    What happens if (just) one of the code reviewers is totally not happy about some part of the change?

  Code Quality

    How do you deal with bugs, what are the priorities, and how they are assessed?

    How do you deal with technical debt? Who is responsible to prioritize it?

    Are your tests fast? Are they reliable? Are they trustworthy?

    How do you document the code?

    How do you deal with badly documented, poorly covered with tests, unreadable code that is below your average?

    How do you deal with legacy code?

    How do you deal with complicated database queries, do you use raw SQL, advanced ORM features, or stored procedures?

    What checks are performed locally and on CI?

  Open Source

    Are you a consumer only, or do you pay back in some way to the Open Source community?

    How motivated your employees are to work on open source during working hours.

    Is it only allowed to work on open source during working hours on things that are blocking or complicating the daily job?

    Is it only allowed to work on open source during working hours on projects that are directly or semi-related to the daily job?

    Do you understand the importance and benefits of sharing the code and practices with the public?

    Are there some dedicated open-source hours, days, sprint points, or employees?

    What are the conditions to be met for an employee to work on an open source project during working hours?

  Development Process

    How does a product change request look like when it reaches the developer?

    To what extent is it possible for a developer to affect this change request?

    Do you use some development process?

    Why this one, not X?

    How well the flavour of this development process is documented?

    To what extent a team can make amendments to the development process to fit their needs and improve their pipeline?

    How hard the product pressure is?

  Career Development

    How transparent are performance reviews and rules behind it?

    How do performance reviews affect the paycheck?

    How to go up in terms of your role and responsibilities?

    How do you deal with the turnover?

    How your turnover compares with other companies?

    Do you have a percentile graph of employee engagement length with the company in the engineering department?

    How fast do you grow?

    What does the firing/quitting process looks like?

  (macro)-Services

    How do you locally run dependent services?

    How do you synchronize releases?

    How do you draw the line between those services?

    Is the database between services shared? Do they have dedicated databases? Do they overlap?

    What do you use to imitate inter-service communication?

    How are the inter-service protocols documented?

    How are the inter-service communications tested?

  Microservices

    Alright, you use (or move to from a monolithic application) microservices.

    What problems did you have you decided to solve with microservices?

    Have you considered alternative approaches to those problems?

    How do you deal with the problems of:
     - consistency
     - availability
     - M*N emitter-consumer communication (note: "the bus" is not an answer)
     - message log retention
     - message schema versioning
     - message-table dualism
     - keeping an actual dimension of data
     - data store separation
     - inter-service join queries
     - deploy synchronization
     - transactional atomic updates of data spread over multiple services (sagas)

  Testing

    Do you perform manual testing?

    Is there a dedicated QA department?

    How thorough is the manual testing?

    When manual testing is happening?

    What do you cover with automated UI tests?

    Do you perform automated load testing?

    Do you perform penetration testing?

  DevOps

    Do regular developers have access to production logs and console?

    Who is responsible for deployments?

    How far the deployment is automated?

    How long does the deployment take?

    Are you able to perform a quick loseless rollback?

    Are you able to quickly deploy a hotfix?

    What do you use for zero-time deployment?

    Do you use atomic and fast schema migrations?

    How do you deal with data migrations?

    What if certain data migrations depend on certain schema migrations, and those are all interleaved in the changes coming from the integration branch.

  Incident Management

    Who is receiving production and staging error messages?

    How do they react to those messages?

  Culture

    What is the culture of the company?

    Is there a specific culture in the engineering department?

    To what extent the culture applies to everything that company does in reality?

    What are the exceptions?

    Did you naturally grow the culture, or was it always an inapproachable ideal you are struggling to reach?

    How do you teach people of Why this culture is good, and how to become good at doing it the company way?

    How do you deal with those who makes an unintentional mistake that costs the company its profit or increases maintenance costs?

    Under which circumstances is it possible to work on your own projects (non-commercial, commercial, and profitable) in your spare time?


  Next time you reach out to hire me, please prepare the answers upfront.
